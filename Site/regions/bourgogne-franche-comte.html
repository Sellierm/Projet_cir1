La Bourgogne-Franche-Comté



Presentation of the region :


Bourgogne-Franche-Comté is an administrative region located in the Center-East of France. It is the result of the administrative merger of the two former regions of Burgundy and Franche-Comté. It is the fifth largest of the thirteen regions of metropolitan France and brings together eight departments: Côte-d'Or, Doubs, Haute-Saône, Jura, Nièvre, Saône- et-Loire, the Territoire de Belfort and the Yonne. It's the third-least populated region and the second least densely populated. Its two capitals are Besançon and Dijon. The first accommodates the headquarters of the regional council, the second the regional prefecture.

Important places to visit :


  • Dijon : If there is one city to visit in Burgundy, it is Dijon! Not only for its 13th century Gothic cathedral, but also for its science and biodiversity garden. This botanical garden consists of a museum, a rose garden, an arboretum and a botanical school. Each plant has a small descriptive label (food, medicinal, textile functions, etc.).

  • Hospices de Beaune : These hospices were built in 1443 following the ravages of the plague and the 100 Years' War. The latter lasted 116 years. These Gothic buildings with glazed tiles are jewels of Burgundian architecture. These polychrome slate roofs are a strong symbol of Burgundy. For the anecdote, the large and magnificent Salle des Pôvres, which you will be led to visit, is so called for a reason.

  • The Roche de Solutré : The Roche de Solutré was awarded the Grand Site de France label in 2013. In addition to being one of the largest prehistoric sites in Europe, this site offers a colourful and wild panorama. The area is also ideal for walking, horse riding and scootering. There are five routes of between 4 and 14 kilometres accessible to all travellers. As you climb to the top of the rock, you will see a sea of vines as far as the eye can see.

  • The Abbaye de Fontenay : The site of Fontenay with its Cistercian architecture is a place to visit in Burgundy. Its abbey is located in the commune of Marmagne in the Côte d'Or. Its cloister is a jewel of Romanesque art and its gardens with their abundant vegetation are most harmonious. Soak up the vibrant atmosphere. Fontenay Abbey hosts film shoots, classical music concerts and Fontenay-style events, especially in summer.

  • The Basilique Sainte-Marie-Madeleine : The Basilica of Saint Mary Magdalene is a World Heritage Site. Let yourself be dazzled by its capitals, its mouldings and its amazing light. Masses are held here every day from Monday to Saturday at 12.15 pm. On request, it is possible to meet with a brother or sister and hear his or her testimony on religious life.

  • The Tanlay Castle : This Renaissance castle, classified as a historical monument, could not be ignored! Grandiose and emblematic, the Château de Tanlay was a meeting place for Huguenot leaders during the Wars of Religion. Today, this building welcomes travellers from all over the world. It is very famous. It is famous for its trompe l'oeil gallery, the frescoes in the League Tower and its landscaped garden. In addition, the Château de Tanlay regularly organises events.
