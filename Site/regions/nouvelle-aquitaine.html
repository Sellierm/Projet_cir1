La Nouvelle-Aquitaine



Presentation of the region :


New Aquitaine is a French administrative region, resulting from the merge of the former Aquitaine, Limousin and Poitou-Charentes regions, it was originally called Aquitaine-Limousin-Poitou-Charentes. It includes twelve departments. It forms the largest region in France (metropolitan and overseas combined), with an area larger than that of Austria. Europe's leading agricultural region in terms of turnover, it is also the leading French region in terms of the number of tourist jobs.

Important places to visit :


  • The Bordeaux Wine Route : For those who want to go even further, discover the Bordeaux Wine Route. An itinerary designed to delight budding oenologists and wine lovers alike. Take a tour of the best vineyards in the Gironde, ready to share their secrets and know-how with you.

  • The Pic du Midi de Bigorre : The Pic du Midi de Bigorre is a high mountain rising to almost 2900 metres. Although it is covered with snow in winter, allowing the resorts to welcome skiers, it remains an exceptional place for hiking. The nature is preserved, a little wild at times, but the views it offers are memorable.

  • The Futuroscope in Poitiers : The reputation of Futuroscope is well established. This futuristic theme park has become a must-see in New Aquitaine thanks to its constant evolution. Indeed, always with a view to offering innovative attractions, the park has never stopped creating. A real paradise, fun and surprising, for young and old alike. Not to be missed under any circumstances.

  • Fort Boyard, La Rochelle : A symbol of the Charente and known for the cult TV show that takes place there, Fort Boyard has, against all odds, a rather serious history. Embark on a boat and get as close as possible to the Fort during guided tours. You can also visit from the air, thanks to helicopter tours !

  • The Dune of Pilat : The Dune du Pilat are the highest dunes in Europe, with an altitude of 110 metres. Climb these dunes, which are almost 3 km long, and enjoy the view of the Arcachon basin and the Banc d'Arguin. Activities such as paragliding are organised here to quench your thirst for adventure.

  • The Proumeyssac chasm : In the Périgord Noir is the Gouffre de Proumeyssac, nicknamed the "Crystal Cathedral". This astonishing place is in fact a converted cave, highlighting incredible geological formations. Thanks to the interplay of lights, music and spaces, the natural cavity is dressed up as a show. A real original discovery, you can visit it in two ways: traditional or by descending in a gondola offering a more global view of the place.
