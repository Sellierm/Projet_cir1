La Bretagne



Presentation of the region :


Brittany is both a French region and a historical and cultural entity. It is located in the west of the country. Its capital is Rennes, although Nantes has played an important role in its history. Brittany is one of the strongest cultural regions in France. Its motto is "Kentoc'h mervel eget bezañ saotret", which means death rather than defilement and its anthem is the Bro gozh ma zadoù. The languages spoken are French, Breton and Gallo. The flag of Brittany is the Gwenn ha Du (in French, white and black).

Important places to visit :


  • The Canaux of Bretagne : You know the coastline... what if you changed your perspective? For a few hours, a day or several, the Breton canals are places of shared tranquillity. Captain for a summer, take your little world on a river cruise. On the towpath, cycle from one Petite Cité de Caractère to another. Get out the canoes and kayaks for a trip along the water. With family, friends, on foot, on pedals or by boat, there is joy and life on the canal! Connect with your loved ones, with yourself: board immediately at the heart of the Breton DNA.

  • Concarneau : It is difficult to resist this jewel of a town, set in its fortifications in the heart of Concarneau and one of the most beautiful bays in Brittany. However, if you escape from its lanes and alleys, other charms are revealed, in its fishing port or on its beaches.

  • The Pink Granite Coast : To the north of Lannion, the Pink Granite Coast is famous for its colourful rocks with poetic shapes. A paradise for birds and walkers, it sculpts the coastline with a thousand and one mineral finds.

  • The Golfe du Morbihan : Here, you don't really know if it's the ocean that has entered the countryside or the other way round. Bright lights, islands and islets as if it were raining, pretty seaside houses... here is the Gulf of Morbihan, the pearl of Southern Brittany, labelled Regional Natural Park.
