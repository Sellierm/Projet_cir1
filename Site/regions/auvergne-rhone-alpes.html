L'Auvergne-Rhône-Alpes



Presentation of the region :


Auvergne-Rhône-Alpes is a French administrative region located in the central and eastern parts of southern France. Bringing together the former Auvergne and Rhône-Alpes regions, it is made up of 12 departments and a metropolis with territorial authority status, its capital is Lyon. This new region brings together spaces that are very different from each other, both geographically, as well as sociological, economic, or even cultural, as was already the case for Rhône-Alpes, and, to a lesser extent, for Auvergne. If the former Rhône-Alpes and Auvergne regions each benefit from a coherence linked to the axes of communication and the attraction of their respective metropolis, the new group appears heterogeneous and has aroused strong opposition from certain local elected officials as well as from part of the population when it was created.

Important places to visit :


  • Cantal Volcano : Discover the oldest of the "Auvergne giants" and the largest volcano in Europe that has been dormant for millions of years, Puy Mary. The diversity and beauty of the landscapes make Le Lioran an unmissable starting point for hikes to the summits of this volcano... Two DH mountain bike areas complete the offer, offering 15 trails for all levels. Two ski lifts, including the Plomb du Cantal cable car, provide easy access to the peaks.

  • The Puy-en-Velay : Le Puy-en-Velay is the European capital of the pilgrimage routes to Santiago de Compostela and is an exceptional site. Its 35-hectare protected area with the Cathedral and the Hôtel-Dieu (listed as a World Heritage Site by UNESCO) and the astonishing Chapelle-Saint Michel d'Aiguilhe bear witness to the richness of its architecture and history.

  • The Basilica of Fourvière : Notre-Dame de Fourvière is a historical and cultural landmark and the leading tourist site in the Auvergne-Rhône-Alpes region, with more than 2.5 million visitors each year. The site is not to be missed because of its basilica, classified as a historic monument and listed as a UNESCO World Heritage Site, with its original architecture, its mosaics and exceptional stained glass windows, its panoramic view, its garden, its tours... A lively place to discover and rediscover.

  • The Ideal Palace of the Facteur Cheval : The Ideal Palace is a unique monument in the world, the work of one man, Joseph Ferdinand Cheval. In 1879, he decided to obstinately build an imaginary palace, inspired by nature, magazines and the postcards he distributed. The recognition of his genius came from artists such as Breton, Picasso...

  • Lake Annecy : The Lake Annecy area, which stretches from the Glières plateau to the Pays d'Alby via the pearl of the lake, Talloires Bay, is internationally renowned for the quality of its lake water, its landscapes and its plethora of activities all year round: outdoor activities (water sports, hiking, cycling), heritage visits, in particular to the old town with its canals and its events programme...

  • Chamonix-Mont-Blanc : The Chamonix-Mont-Blanc Valley is a major tourist site in the Alps with the highest point in Western Europe, Mont Blanc, at 4809 m. Montenvers, Brévent, Aiguille du Midi: these emblematic sites have contributed to the birth and development of tourism and mountain activities. Chamonix-Mont-Blanc is a reference territory that has preserved a unique architectural heritage that bears witness to a long and rich tourist history.
