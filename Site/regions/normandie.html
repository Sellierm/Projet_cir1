Normandie



Présentation of the region

During the difficult post-war period, many devastated cities had to be rebuilt, notably Caen and Le Havre. In 1956, France created a new administrative level: the regions. Calvados, Manche and Orne were grouped together in the region of Basse-Normandie, while Eure and Seine-Maritime were grouped together in the region of Haute-Normandie. This separation is not unanimous and the reunification of Normandy, by grouping the five Norman departments, becomes a recurring topic in local politics. Like other French regions, the two regions of Normandy developed their economies through industrial decentralization plans, including the establishment of factories related to the automotive industry. The reunification of the two regions into a single Normandy is enacted on December 17, 2014 for an effective date of January 1, 2016.

Important places to visit :

  • Honfleur : This is the mythical port of Normandy! The one whose high houses are known all over the world and whose village atmosphere still charms as much. After strolling on the harbour, take the time to climb the small alleys to gain height and enjoy a very nice view.

  • Mont Saint Michel : Some claim to be Breton but it is in Normandy that we must go to see it. He first guessed himself in the distance before appearing in all his majesty. The view from the terraces of the abbey is simply magical! Before leaving, we must not fail to contemplate it one last time in the light of the sunset.

  • The Memorial and the landing beaches : Land of memory, it is necessary to see in Normandy, the landing beaches and visit the Memorial, museum dedicated to the history of the twentieth century with the Second World War in the background. The museum, far from being academic, proposes several scenographies to reflect on the stakes of peace while The Gardens of Remembrance commemorate the commitment of allied nations.

  • The Camembert Road : It is the symbol of France in the world! It is therefore impossible to visit the region without tasting it and learn more about this must-see of Normandy. It’s time for a mini road trip in the Orne in the footsteps of the famous cheese through beautiful bucolic landscapes. And, luckily, the cider road runs through the corner as well.
