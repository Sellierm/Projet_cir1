Le Grand-Est



Presentation of the region :


The Grand Est is the region resulting from the fusion between Alsace, Champagne Ardennes, and Lorraine. The biggest city is Strasbourg and it's also the chief town. The region is sharing a Latin tradition and a Germanic tradition. The region also has a great industrial dimension and is also bordered by Germany, Belgium, Luxembourg, and Swiss.

Specialities :


  • The Boudin of Nancy
  • The Bouneschlupp
  • The choucroute
  • The flammekueche
  • The kouglof

Important places to visit :


  • The old town of Colmar : Often referred to as the "Little Venice", Colmar is one of the most beautiful towns in the former Alsace. It overlooks the Lauch River and the Logelbach Canal, much like its Italian counterpart. But what makes Colmar so charming is certainly its atypical architecture.

  • The old town of Troyes : Troyes is obviously famous for its emblematic cathedral. But this is obviously not the only asset of this exceptional town! The city is indeed characterised by its magnificent traditional architecture. Between the cobbled streets and half-timbered houses, you will have the impression of taking a real leap back in time.

  • Sundgau : At the crossroads of Switzerland, Germany and Franche-Comté, the Sundgau is full of exceptional landscapes. This small territory, a few kilometres from the Jura, is studded with green and wild valleys. A real paradise for cycling enthusiasts, it invites you to explore its natural expanses.

  • The Alsace wine route : Whether you are a wine lover or not, taking a wine route is an excellent way to visit a place. The Alsace Grand Cru route is almost 170 km long and passes through some of the region's beautiful villages. Starting from Colmar or Strasbourg, you will discover the splendid Alsatian scenery and an exceptional culture !
