L'Occitanie



Presentation of the region :


Occitania is a very young region. Many still say "Midi-Pyrénées" or "Languedoc-Roussillon", because Occitania was born from the merger in 2016 of these two major regions. Occitania is now among the top 3 French tourist destinations. It brings together all the holiday worlds: the countryside, the mountains, the sea and the city with, of course.

Specialities :


  • The Chasselas grape from Moissac
  • Pescajoune du Quercy
  • The garbure
  • The aligot
  • The Pérail
  • The Fénétra of Toulouse

Important places to visit :


  • The city of Carcassonne : The medieval walled city of Carcassonne is a must-see in Occitania. Its 3 kilometers of ramparts and its 52 towers are visible for miles around. The city is also the most famous example of the work carried out by Viollet-Le-Duc in the 19th century to rehabilitate medieval buildings. You can admire the Saint-Nazaire Basilica, the Comtal Castle, walk on the lices or simply stroll in the old town. The only drawback is that you will have to deal with the hordes of tourists. .

  • The Mount Canigó: It is the most famous peak of the Eastern Pyrenees with its 2784 meters high. You can go on many hikes and climb to its summit if you are a confirmed sportsman. If not, many more accessible walks await you in superb landscapes, without forgetting the Saint-Martin du Canigo abbey built on a rocky peak.

  • The jousts of Sète : Bordered by the Thau Lagoon and the Mediterranean Sea, Sète is a city prized by visitors and artists! Its museums, its sandy beaches, its architecture and its gastronomy have made the city a must-see in Occitania.
    But, if there is one thing you must see in Sète, it is the nautical jousts. Organized from June to September, they follow very precise rules and ceremonial before the fight is engaged. Place your bets!

  • The Cathar castles :It is impossible to visit Occitania without visiting the Cathar fortresses! Quéribus, Montségur, Peyrepertuse, you must have heard of these castles perched on rocky peaks several hundred meters high. Reputedly impregnable, some of them had a tragic history during the crusade against the Albigensians, known as Cathars, who were a group of dissident believers of Catholicism in the 13th century. One thing is for sure, exploring them with the wind blowing through the ruins is an unforgettable experience.
