L'Ile de France



Presentation of the region :


The Ile de France, formerly known as the Paris region, is the most populated administrative region in France. It includes Paris and seven neighbouring departments with an area of 12 011kmsup>2/sup> The island of France has many assets such as its GDP (Gross Domestic Product)which represents 31% of the national wealth, its strong industrial tradition, its networks of high-speed communication and its ports (2sup>ème/sup> European river platform). Moreover, the island of France is the first tourist region in the world. It welcomes 50 million visitors per year. the Region has a fund dedicated to the sector to support tourism professionals in their projects of development and renewal of their offer. With the health crisis, the Regional Fund is stepping up its efforts to enable tourism professionals to adapt to the changing needs and expectations of customers, particularly in terms of reinsurance and digital transition.

Important places to visit :


  • The Louvre : The Louvre is home to 35,000 works of art on display on more than 60,600 m² and ten million visitors a year. The Louvre Museum is huge and extremely popular. Let’s say you won’t be able to see everything in a day, but these skip-the-line tickets will allow you to enter directly without wasting time waiting! Book your tickets and target the collections and departments you are interested in:

    • Egyptian antiques
    • Oriental antiques
    • Greek, Etruscan and Roman Antiquities
    • Islamic art
    • Sculptures
    • Decorative Arts
    • Painting
    • Engravings and drawings

  • Eiffel Tower : After a brief introduction in English on the history and construction of the tower, you will be accompanied to the security checks up to the elevator. Coin-operated binoculars are available to get up close to certain monuments and sites such as Notre-Dame, the Sacré Coeur in Montmartre, the Arc de Triomphe, the Trocadéro and much more.
    On the program:
    • Unlimited time to see the city
    • The vertiginous glass floor on the first floor
    • Second floor information windows and interactive screens
    • Two-story restaurants with great views
    • Optional: Standard access to the summit with stunning views, a replica of Gustave Eiffel’s office and a high-end champagne bar

  • Versailles Palace (park) : The grandeur of this park represents the grandeur of an era that of the Sun King. To refresh your memory, remember that it was Louis XIV who gave the order to build this sumptuous palace as well as all this green setting.
    The Versailles Park or called the Gardens of Versailles was designed by André Le Nôtre according to a manual written by the king himself, who wanted only one thing with these gardens: to show control over nature.
    It is now possible to visit them during your visit to the Palace of Versailles or separately.
    In order to create intimate places for his feasts, the king had built sheltered places called groves.
    Often inspired by mythology, the groves allow you to find rest and freshness. There are a series of fountains, water installations and statues.
    You will also see fountains and statues in mythologically inspired architecture.
    Further on, the Estate of Trianon will bring you face to face with royalty as it was the resting place of the sovereigns with these vast glades for walking.
    The Petit Trianon keeps the memory of Marie-Antoinette. Finally, in the Grand Parc, there are vast glades for walking.
    Before your departure, find out more about the water show and the timetable. It is a pure moment of happiness to walk in this garden with the sweet sounds of the groves.
    In the summer, there are also sound and light shows in the evening, very interesting..
